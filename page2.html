Computer Networks

Computer networks are systems that connect multiple devices, such as computers, servers, and routers, to share data and resources. They enable communication and collaboration through wired or wireless connections, forming local area networks (LANs) or expansive networks like the internet.

Personal Area Network

    A personal area network (PAN) connects electronic devices within a user's immediate area. The size of a PAN ranges from a few centimeters to a few meters. One of the most common real-world examples of a PAN is the connection between a Bluetooth earpiece and a smartphone

    Local Area Network

      A local area network (LAN) is a collection of devices connected together in one physical location, such as a building, office, or home. A LAN can be small or large, ranging from a home network with one user to an enterprise network with thousands of users and devices in an office or school.

Metropolitan Area Network

A metropolitan area network (MAN) is a computer network that connects computers within a metropolitan area, which could be a single large city, multiple cities and towns, or any given large area with multiple buildings. A MAN is larger than a local area network (LAN) but smaller than a wide area network (WAN).

wide area network

    A wide area network (WAN), is a large network of information that is not tied to a single location. WANs can facilitate communication, the sharing of information and much more between devices from around the world through a WAN provider.

"Wired/Wireless Communication">
    Wired: Uses cables to connect devices. It's usually faster and more secure. Wireless: Uses radio waves to connect devices. It's more convenient but can be slower and less secure.

Wifi

Wi-Fi is a wireless networking technology that allows devices such as computers (laptops and desktops), mobile devices (smart phones and wearables),and other equipment (printers and video cameras) to interface with the Internet.

bluetooth

  • Bluetooth is a short-range wireless technology standard that is used for exchanging data between fixed and mobile devices over short distances and building personal area networks. In the most widely used mode,transmission power is limited to 2.5 milliwatts, giving it a very short range of up to 10 metres

Cloud computers (private/public)

    PRIVATE CLOUD: a service that is completely controlled by a single organization and not shared with others. PUBLIC CLOUD: is a subscription service that is also offered to any and all customers who want similar services

Virus

A virus is a type of malicious software that targets devices like computers, phones, and laptops, often through email, infected websites, or unknown downloads. Its primary goal is to steal personal information, such as social security details, bank accounts, or identities, which can be used for blackmail, fraud, or public humiliation. Viruses can also damage devices internally, causing slow system performance, random pop-ups, or unexpected logouts. Staying vigilant and avoiding suspicious links or downloads is essential to protect against such threats.

Malware

  • Malware is a type of software designed to target internet-connected devices, aiming to disrupt, damage, or gain unauthorized access to systems to steal personal information. Common types of malware include viruses, which disrupt device functionality; trojan horses, which disguise themselves as legitimate software or websites; and spyware, which secretly collects personal data. Protecting devices from malware requires caution and the use of reliable security measures.
